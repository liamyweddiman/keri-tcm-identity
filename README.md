# KERI-TCM-Identity  
### A KERI-based Identity Framework for TCM Practitioners & Traditional Medicine Provenance  
**Author: Liam YW Eddiman**

---

## üîç Overview
This repository explores how **KERI (Key Event Receipt Infrastructure)** can support a trustworthy, verifiable identity infrastructure for the Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) ecosystem.

The project focuses on two core domains:

1. **TCM Practitioner Credential Governance**
   - Apprenticeship & lineage attestation  
   - Institutional training & certification records  
   - Cross-border medical license verification  
   - Decentralized clinical activity & renewal logs  

2. **Traditional Medicine & Herbal Product Provenance**
   - Farm-level origin authentication  
   - Processing & extraction event chains  
   - Laboratory testing & batch attestation  
   - Export certification & pharmaceutical integration  

---

## üß¨ Motivation
Traditional medicine systems face persistent challenges:

- Inconsistent credentialing standards across countries  
- Difficulty verifying lineage and apprenticeship claims  
- Counterfeit or unclear-origin herbal products  
- Weak international interoperability of medical licenses  

**KERI‚Äôs event-based identifier model** provides a secure, auditable foundation for solving these problems by enabling cryptographically verifiable histories of identity, skills, and product provenance.

## üß± KERI Identity Architecture (Concept)
### Practitioner Identity Event Chain
- `Inception` ‚Äì Practitioner AID creation  
- `Lineage Attestation` ‚Äì Master/institution endorsements  
- `Training Record Events` ‚Äì Hospitals, colleges, or programs  
- `License Issuance` ‚Äì Local or national medical boards  
- `Cross-border License Binding` ‚Äì AU/EU/US/ASEAN equivalency  
- `Renewal / Discipline Events`  
- `Revocation` (if applicable)  

### Herbal Product Provenance Chain
- `Origin Event` ‚Äì Farm/region batch AID  
- `Harvest` ‚Äì Date, geo-location, variety  
- `Processing Attestation` ‚Äì Drying, extraction, compounding  
- `Laboratory QC Events` ‚Äì Purity, safety, heavy metal testing  
- `Packaging & Lot Creation`  
- `Export Certification`  
- `Pharmaceutical Manufacturer Receipt`
